CIDCustomer Induced Damage (CID) A repeat repair event is required to address damage caused by the customer, unrelated to the previous work order
Customer Interference Initially, the customer did not allow the repair to proceed, but has now agreed to the repair
Customer Unavailable The customer was unavailable at the scheduled time for the initial repair attempt
Unsuitable Usage Environment The customer’s environment was unsuitable for the repair, preventing the technician from completing the work
Incorrect Action Plan The Customer Engagement Center (CEC) team failed to diagnose the unit comprehensively, leading to an incomplete repair
Additional Failure Identified During the onsite visit, the engineer discovered additional issues that were not apparent during the initial diagnosis
Workmanship Issue The engineer’s actions, such as causing System Induced Damage (SID), using incorrect tools, or performing an incorrect repair, led to the need for a repeat repair
ECA / CritSitEngineering Change Announcement (ECA) / Critical Situation A repeat repair is required due to an Engineering Change Announcement (ECA) or a suggestion from the regional Level 2 support team
Lenovo Software Issue A BIOS or firmware update during or after the initial repair caused part failure
Part DOADead on Arrival (DOA) The part delivered to the customer or engineer was defective upon arrival
Damage in Transport The part or equipment was damaged while being transported to the customer or engineer
Previous WO Cancelled The prior work order was cancelled before it could be Released
Previous WO NTF/SBUNo Trouble Found (NTF) / Service By User (SBU) During the site visit, the engineer found no issues with the unit, indicating a No Trouble Found (NTF) situation
Other / Unknown Choose the “Other/Unknown” category only if none of the predefined options apply to the situation
Unrelated Product Failure The current problem is unrelated to the previous repair or part replacement
Part Delivery Issue The delivery partner failed to deliver the part to the customer or engineer
Repair Following Onsite Troubleshooting The correct part was sent by the CEC, but during the repair activity, the engineer identified the root cause and found a different part causing the issue
